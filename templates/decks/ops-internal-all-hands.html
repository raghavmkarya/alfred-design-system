05
Trust guardrails

Keeping our confidence honest

Higher is better
Recommendation acceptance rate

The share of recommendations a leader accepts and acts on. If they don't act, we haven't earned the decision.

Lower is better
Calibration error

Does a stated Causal Confidence Score match what actually happened? Confidence only counts if it's honest.

Target: zero
Wrong-confident recommendations

High-confidence calls that turned out wrong. The one number we never let drift above zero.

Every hypothesis Alfred makes carries a Causal Confidence Score from the Causal Reasoning Engine. These three guardrails keep that score worth trusting.
