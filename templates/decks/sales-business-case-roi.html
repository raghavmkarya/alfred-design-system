The CFO's problem

Marketing has been a black box.

Spend isn't tied to revenue. Budget goes out across 5–15 tools; what came back is reconstructed by hand, weeks later.

You approve on trust, not evidence. The narrative arrives after the quarter closes — too late to change the outcome.

No audit trail on decisions. When the board asks "why did we spend there?", the answer lives in someone's memory.
