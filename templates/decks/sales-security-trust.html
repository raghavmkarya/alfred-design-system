The objection

02
The principle

Trust, by architecture.

Every safeguard that follows is a design decision, not a policy promise — read-only access, data isolation and no cross-customer training are built into how Alfred works.

03
How access works

Read-only by default. Write only when you say so.

1
Connect read-only
Secure read-only API links to your stack. Alfred can see your data; it cannot change it.
2
Encrypt
Every connection and synced record is encrypted in transit and at rest. No exceptions.
3
Authorise write-back
Acting inside your tools stays off until you explicitly grant it — per integration.
4
Log everything
Every read, recommendation and write lands in a full, exportable audit trail.
04
Data governance

Four lines that don't move

Data isolation

Each customer's data lives in its own isolated boundary. No shared tables, no commingling across accounts.

Private pattern library

The patterns Alfred learns about your business stay in your tenant. They are yours, and only yours.

No cross-customer training

Your data never trains another customer's Alfred — or any shared foundational model. Ever.

API-only model usage

Foundational models are reached through APIs only. No third-party data sharing, no data sold on.

05
Audit & access

Who did what, when — always on the record

Full audit trail

Every read, recommendation and write-back is logged with who, what and when — exportable for review at any time.

Role-based access

RBAC on Max and Enterprise. People see and do only what their role permits — nothing further.

Authorised write-back only

Alfred acts in your tools only after you explicitly authorise it, per integration. Write-back is encrypted, and every action is recorded.
