Drowning in dashboards
Every tool in your stack ships its own dashboard. Ads, analytics, CRM, lifecycle, billing — each one accurate, each one incomplete, none of them talking to each other. So the morning turns into tab-hopping: read eight reports, reconcile three versions of the truth, and hope you caught the thing that mattered before your first meeting.
18
data sources the average marketing team expected to juggle in 2023 — up from 10 in 2021. The reading load nearly doubled in two years.
Source: Salesforce, State of Marketing report, 8th edition (2022) · survey of 6,000 marketers worldwide
More sources mean more dashboards, and more dashboards mean more reading. The problem was never missing data — you have more of it than any team in history. The problem is that nobody's job is to read all of it, remember all of it, and say what to do next.
That gap has a name now: decision intelligence — a layer that detects what changed, explains why, and recommends what to do next. Not another dashboard, not another chatbot, not another analytics tool.
