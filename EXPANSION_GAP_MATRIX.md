## Purpose

This matrix compares the visual journey required by each flagship-launch audience with the current
tracked design-system capability. It distinguishes an existing base from a launch-ready system.

No channel is marked launch-ready because campaign content binding, manifests, approval states,
embargo controls, and deterministic verified exports do not exist yet.

## Legend

- **Revise:** a substantial current master exists, but it needs campaign binding, positioning,
  placement, proof, or lifecycle work.
- **Partial:** useful primitives or adjacent templates exist, but the audience-specific composition
  is incomplete.
- **Missing:** no dedicated visual system exists for the audience and channel.
- **N/A:** the channel is not required for the flagship audience journey.

## Audience findings

### General market and category learners

Current strength:

- The decision-intelligence category is established across pages, decks, and social frames.
- Category language is largely aligned with the naming canon.
- Dark and light social parity is strong.

Missing launch capability:

- No campaign content source or manifest connects the category story across channels.
- LinkedIn masters use an upload-compatible 8:9 shape instead of the current recommended 4:5 paid
  vertical shape.
- No category-reveal motion system exists.
- No press-ready category explainer or media-kit narrative exists.

Primary tasks: EP-101 through EP-112, EP-202, EP-302, EP-306, EP-403, EP-603, EP-701, EP-702.

### Prospects and waitlist

Current strength:

- Product-marketing, pricing, waitlist, contact, nurture, launch-offer, social, sales, and collateral
  masters provide broad funnel coverage.

Missing launch capability:

- The 50% introductory offer is duplicated across six masters.
- Product proof is not organized as the required 12-shot, responsive crop system.
- Social frames lack campaign manifests and exact placement-safe exports.
- No coordinated short-form launch motion or product-proof retargeting family exists.

Primary tasks: EP-303, EP-308, EP-309, EP-315, EP-401, EP-402, EP-406, EP-704, EP-707, EP-801
through EP-803.

### Customers

Current strength:

- Product brief, alert, onboarding, digest, QBR, pilot, and customer-success patterns exist.
- The product and lifecycle voice is established.

Missing launch capability:

- Several customer-facing examples contain unapproved outcome patterns.
- No customer-specific public launch announcement, advocacy system, or post-launch milestone family
  exists.
- Product screenshots are not governed by current-product accuracy and responsive crop metadata.

Primary tasks: EP-309, EP-313, EP-703, EP-707, EP-708, EP-714, EP-801, EP-802.

## Sequencing conclusion

The gap matrix supports the milestone order already defined in the program:

1. Build campaign content, manifests, states, citations, safe zones, and primitives.
2. Produce static social, website, product, and illustration masters.
3. Build investor, press, and confidential funding systems.
4. Add motion and poster-frame variants.
5. Verify and approve the complete flagship pack.

Starting with additional isolated frames would not close an audience journey. The first production
work should establish the shared content and manifest contracts that every audience row depends on.
